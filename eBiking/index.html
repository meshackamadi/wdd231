🌿 Urban Pedal Project

domain: urbanpedal.coop

Why this name? “Urban Pedal Project” reflects a community-driven initiative focused on city cycling, sustainable commuting, and inclusive bike culture. Urban connects directly to city riders, while Pedal Project evokes action, mission, and collaboration. The name is modern, memorable, and scalable — perfect for a cycling hub offering route intelligence, gear reviews, and local events.

📌 Site Purpose

The Urban Pedal Project website serves as a central digital hub for urban commuters, recreational cyclists, and e-bike enthusiasts. The platform provides curated bike route reviews, safety & maintenance tutorials, a community events calendar, and an interactive gear recommendation system. Visitors can also access a membership newsletter sign-up, local advocacy group connections, and expert Q&A. The purpose is to empower riders of all skill levels with trustworthy information while fostering a vibrant, eco-friendly cycling community.

🧭 User Scenarios

⚡
“Best commuter e-bike for hilly neighborhoods?”

Leo lives in a steep district and wants to replace car commutes with an e-bike. He visits the site seeking torque ratings, battery range data, and real-world hill-climb tests. The site delivers a dedicated “E-Bike Comparator” with expert reviews, budget picks, and safety scores.

🗺️
“Where can I find safe bike lanes near downtown?”

Maya is a beginner cyclist new to the city. She needs low-stress routes and current protected lane info. The platform provides an interactive route library, downloadable PDF maps, and user-rated safety metrics — answering her needs instantly.

📅
“How do I join monthly group rides or contact organizers?”

Carlos wants community connection. The site includes a dynamic “Ride Calendar,” direct contact form for club directors, and integrated social links, giving him a clear path to involvement and direct communication.

🎨 Color Scheme

The selected palette is actively applied throughout this site plan — headings, backgrounds, borders, and accent elements follow the defined scheme.

#1E5F5F · Forest Teal Primary heading color, key UI elements, navigation accents
#E3A56D · Warm Terracotta Accent color: underlines, left borders, badges, interactive highlights
#F9F6EF · Soft Cream Page background, card backgrounds, subtle contrast areas
#1E2F2F · Deep Teal-Gray Primary body text, readable paragraphs, footer text
✔ Practical usage: Headings use Forest Teal (#1E5F5F), body text Deep Teal-Gray (#1E2F2F), Terracotta (#E3A56D) for borders and decorative highlights, Soft Cream (#F9F6EF) for ambient backgrounds to ensure approachable, high-contrast readability.

✍️ Typography

Playfair Display

→ Used for all headings (h1, h2, h3) and the site name. Elegant serif that conveys authority, editorial quality, and warmth.

Inter

→ Primary body text, paragraphs, lists, navigation, and UI components. Clean, modern sans-serif with superior legibility on all devices.

JetBrains Mono

→ Reserved for technical accents, domain names, scenario labels, code-like specs (e-bike data). Adds distinctive character without overuse.

✓ Exactly three fonts selected: Playfair Display (headings), Inter (body), JetBrains Mono (specialized). This maintains consistency, performance, and professional brand identity.
